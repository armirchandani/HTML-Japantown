Kabuki Theatre

Kabuki theatre in San Francisco's Japantown offers a unique cultural experience, blending traditional Japanese performance art with modern adaptations. Known for its elaborate costumes, stylized acting, and dramatic storytelling, Kabuki showcases Japan's rich theatrical heritage. Performances in Japantown often serve as a bridge between cultures, introducing audiences to this art form's vibrant history and captivating visual appeal.

Benihana

Benihana is a popular Japanese restaurant with locations in many states. The name means Red flower in Japanese.Benihana prepares entertaining meals teppanyaki-style on a large grill right in front of you. They also serve sushi, but there is not action in sushi. As they put it: “We serve great American favorites like sirloin, chicken and fresh seafood. All according to a 1000 year-old Japanese recipe”. We decided to give it another visit to show all the theatrics to an uninitiated member of our party.

Pagoda

A pagoda is a multi-story tower with multiple roofs that is often used for religious purposes in East Asia. Pagodas are often highly decorated and are commonly found in China, Japan, Thailand, Cambodia, Nepal, Korea, Myanmar, and Vietnam. They were originally built as memorials or shrines, and are most often Buddhist but can also be Taoist.
